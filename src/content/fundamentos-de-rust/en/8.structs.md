# Structs

Structs are custom data types that group related fields under a name. They are the equivalent of classes in other languages, though without inheritance.

In Rust, the logic associated with a struct is defined separately in `impl` blocks, which keeps data definitions and behavior clearly separated.

This chapter covers:

- Defining structs and creating instances
- Tuple structs and unit structs
- `impl` blocks: methods and associated functions
- `self`, `&self`, `&mut self`
- Struct update syntax
- Struct exercise
